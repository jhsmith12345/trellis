#### Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP)

PARTIAL HOSPITALIZATION PROGRAM (PHP)
Receive comprehensive treatment in a structured environment. Our Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP) provides inpatient-level treatment while allowing clients to return home or sober living.
PHP group therapy sessions typically last six hours a day for 14-28 days. For the best outcome, we typically recommend those in PHP reside in transitional housing to help clients easily transition back into society.
INTENSIVE OUTPATIENT PROGRAM (IOP)
Rebuild your life while recovering in a supportive environment. Our Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP) allows clients to practice the principles of recovery in a community setting with fewer restrictions than PHP.
IOP is a step down from PHP on our continuum of care, and it helps clients recover while reintegrating into their community. IOP group therapy sessions are held in three-hour blocks, 3-5 times per week. As with PHP, combining transitional housing with IOP contributes to lasting recovery.
OUTPATIENT PROGRAM (OP)
Receive long-term support for your recovery journey. Our Outpatient Program (OP) provides addiction treatment sessions for clients reintegrating into their lives.
OP helps clients sustain their recovery progress. Ongoing individual and psychiatric sessions reflect our commitment to safety, community, and accountability while also enabling clients to live their private lives.

#### What Is Trauma?

WHAT IS TRAUMA?
Trauma is subjective and unique to each individual. Two people can be involved in the same car wreck but have entirely different experiences over it.
At its core, trauma is a normal biological response to a distressing event or experience. Trauma overwhelms someone’s ability to cope with the occurrence, decreases the ability to function,  and causes emotional turmoil. Trauma also reduces logical and rational processing in one’s brain.

#### Overcome Addiction Triggers Caused by Trauma

OVERCOME ADDICTION TRIGGERS CAUSED BY TRAUMA
Trauma may cause distressing yet instinctive reactions to triggering events. In response, individuals may self-medicate with drugs or alcohol, developing addiction along the way.
At Stages of Recovery, we provide trauma informed care and trauma specific treatment for individuals whose addiction treatment plans call for it. Our Certified Clinical Trauma Professionals (CCTP) incorporate trauma therapies — including trigger management, coping skills, and stabilization — to support lifelong sobriety.
HOW STAGES OF RECOVERY ADDRESSES TRAUMA
Stages of Recovery’s staff members train in EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing). EMDR centers around bringing the logical, rational side of the brain back “online.” Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing has risen in popularity over the previous years and claims an 83% success rate. Aside from EMDR, we provide Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy and prescribe medications to help patients overcome addiction and trauma.
Our staff consists of Certified Clinical Trauma Professionals (CCTP), an on-staff Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioners, with Masters-Level Counselors available.
The time spent in trauma treatment varies by patient, and we incorporate a patient’s history into a personalized trauma treatment plan. While our primary focus is on treating substance abuse, we filter addiction through the lens of trauma therapy for those who need it.
Trauma informed care and trauma treatment is intense. It is not to be taken lightly, especially in the outpatient setting. As such, our focus during early treatment is primarily on substance use, trigger management, coping skills, relapse prevention, and stabilization. We help patients work on these factors and become stable before moving into more intense trauma specific treatment.

#### Education

EDUCATION
Education has always been a pillar of recovery at Stages. We combine formal and informal education opportunities during treatment. Through community development and relationships built with our staff, clients witness the strength and hope of others in recovery. That education continues through a structured 90-day clinical outpatient program. By teaching the disease of addiction and its impact, life skills development, and accountability, we are changing the way clients recover from a life of substance abuse.

#### Family Support Study

FAMILY SUPPORT STUDY
Stages of Recovery participates in an ongoing research study affiliated with Texas Tech University and the University of Connecticut. Those with a young adult (age 18-30) in substance use treatment at Stages of Recovery-Lubbock are invited to attend an eight-week workshop based on mindfulness, called PRISM: Promoting Resilience in Self-Management.
Workshops are currently held in Lubbock, TX, at the Stages of Recovery Clinical Building on Saturdays from 10 am-11 am. Participation is voluntary and confidential.
Caregivers for those struggling with substance use experience undeniable emotional and physical distress. This clinician-led workshop provides an opportunity to hear the stories of others who are supporting their loved ones in recovery. During the eight weeks, family and friends will learn the skills needed to:
Manage stress and practice self-care
Implement constructive ways to interact with the individual in treatment
Cope with life circumstances
If you would like to join the workshop, please email melissa.silva@stagesofrecovery.net to discuss your participation!
CLIENT FAMILY SUPPORT GROUP
Stages of Recovery offers a virtual family-support meeting via Zoom each Tuesday at 7:00 pm. Each meeting is led by our staff member and long-time recovery advocate, Mechie Scherpereel. At Stages, we understand recovery isn’t just about one person – addiction isn’t an island. Our Family Support Group ensures that everyone in the wake of addiction moves forward to heal through this treatment journey and beyond.
This non-clinical setting allows a client’s family and loved ones to share their experience, strength, and hope. It also safely lets family members process the physical and emotional storm experienced during their loved one’s addiction in a productive and solution-focused environment. PSYCHIATRIC EVALUATIONS

PRIMARY MENTAL HEALTH TREATMENT
While Stages of Recovery is not a primary mental health provider, our Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner is skilled in dual diagnosis treatment.
SPECIALIZED IN DUAL DIAGNOSIS
Addiction to drugs and alcohol doesn’t often exist in isolation. In fact, it is common for individuals to have dual diagnosis and suffer from both addiction and mental illness. Dual diagnosis can be particularly difficult, influencing every aspect of one’s life and leaving one’s perceptions vulnerable. While difficult, all is not lost: dual diagnosis is treatable. With the proper care, many dual diagnosis clients recover and integrate into their communities.
Stages of Recovery specializes in dual diagnosis care. Between our licensed counselors and on staff Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner, Stages is capable of both managing a variety of dual diagnosis conditions and prescribing proper medication.
